# Design Backlog

Deferred from the April 2026 design review. Revisit during future design sweeps.

## Ideas

- **Home-page bio cue.** A cold visitor learns nothing about who's writing without clicking "About." Consider a small byline ("Director of Content and Digital Services at the Boston Symphony Orchestra") or a single-sentence pull from `/about`, positioned below the hero or as a footer to the recent-posts list. *Why:* converts landings from socials; gives the blog a face.

- **Featured-post differentiation.** Post listings render identically — no visual weight on the latest or flagship piece. Consider a richer card (cover image, longer excerpt, larger title) for the most recent post or a manually-flagged "essay of note." *Why:* steers readers toward the strongest piece.

- **Footer polish.** Current footer is one row (copyright + social + code + RSS). Consider a one-sentence mission above the copyright and an explicit label on the RSS icon. *Why:* footer is real estate on a thought-leadership blog; right now it says nothing.

- **Post-page "← Back" treatment.** The top-of-post back link is orphaned and duplicates the sticky header's home nav + the prev/next at the bottom. Either remove it or integrate it typographically into the byline row. *Why:* reduces visual noise at the top of every post.

- **Dark mode decision.** The cream main-bg is a deliberate light-theme choice. Either lean in (add texture/grain, commit to light-only) or build a proper second dark aesthetic (not a token swap — a distinct visual voice). *Why:* avoid a half-committed dark mode that dilutes the light one.

- **Reading paths.** Tags, series, and related posts exist but feel like afterthoughts. Consider an end-of-post "more on this topic" module or a proper series index page. *Why:* helps readers go deeper without leaving.
